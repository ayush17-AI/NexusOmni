## BGIS (BGMI India Series) 2025 Rules

**Format**
• Open qualifiers → State-level qualifiers → National playoffs → Grand Finals
• Teams of 4 players compete in Battle Royale matches on rotation across Erangel, Miramar, and Sanhok
• Each Grand Finals event consists of 6 matches per day across 2 days (12 matches total)

**Eligibility**
• Players must hold Indian citizenship
• All players must be 16 years or older
• Teams must be registered entities with formal rosters submitted before the qualifier deadline

**Match Rules**
• 3rd-party interference is penalised
• Moving out of the safe zone deliberately to eliminate another team is considered unsportsmanlike
• Teaming (cooperating with an enemy squad) results in immediate disqualification

## BMPS (BGMI Pro Series) 2025 Rules

**Format**
• League phase: 6 weeks of regular matches among 24 invited pro teams
• Playoffs: Top 16 teams advance, played over 2 weekends
• Finals: Top 12 teams, 6 matches per day, 2 days

**Seeding**
• Teams invited based on performance in previous BGIS or BMPS seasons
• Wild-card slots available through open qualifiers

## BGMI Esports Scoring System (10-Point System)

**Placement Points**
| Rank  | Points |
|-------|--------|
| #1    | 10     |
| #2    | 6      |
| #3    | 5      |
| #4    | 4      |
| #5    | 3      |
| #6-10 | 2      |
| #11-15| 1      |
| #16+  | 0      |

**Kill Points**
• Each elimination = 1 point
• No cap on kill points per match

**Total Score = Placement Points + Kill Points**

This scoring system rewards both aggressive playstyle (fragging) and survivability.
A team that places #1 with 0 kills scores 10 points.
A team that places #5 with 10 kills scores 13 points.

## Player Roles in BGMI Esports

**IGL (In-Game Leader)**
• Makes macro-level decisions: rotations, circle play, engagement timing
• Calls when to push, hold, or retreat
• Must maintain consistent game sense and situational awareness
• Should NOT be the primary fragger — focus is on decision-making
• Early game: decides drop location, initial loot distribution

**Fragger / Entry Fragger**
• First to push enemy positions
• High mechanical skill, fast reflexes, aggressive playstyle
• Typically uses AR + SMG for close-range and mid-range
• Role in ranked/pro play: create space and take first blood on pushes

**Support**
• Provides utility: smoke grenades, flashbangs, stun coverage
• Revives teammates in fight
• Carries med kits, bandages, painkillers, and energy drinks for team
• Holds flanks and safe angles

**Scout**
• Gathers intel on enemy positions without direct engagement
• Uses 4x-6x scopes for long-range spotting
• Calls enemy count, equipment level, and rotation direction to IGL
• Often uses bolt-action sniper (Kar98, M24) for early picks

## Erangel Map Strategy

**Key Drop Locations**
• Military Base (MILBASE): High-tier loot but very contested. Commit fully or avoid.
• Georgopol: Crates and containers with reliable mid-range loot.
• Pochinki: Central location, good loot, useful for circle play.
• Yasnaya Polyana: Large city, excellent loot spread.
• School (Sosnovka): High-risk, classic hot drop for aggressive teams.

**Rotation Strategy — Circle to Military Base (MILBASE)**
• If circle pulls to MILBASE from Georgopol or north:
  - Teams should take the bridge early (bridge fight is common)
  - Alternatively, boat rotation via Sosnovka river is safer but slower
  - Hold Sosnovka hill elevation before the bridge for oversight
• From Pochinki to MILBASE:
  - Rotate early through the south to avoid bridge congestion
  - Take vehicles (UAZ/Dacia) and abandon them before entering compound
  - Enter MILBASE from south hill for elevation advantage

**Zone Play Tips**
• Always be inside the blue zone edge when whitezone shrinks — don't rush to center
• High ground = control in Erangel's open terrain
• Final circles often settle near Pochinki, Mylta Power, or Farm
• Teams that rotate late but have vehicles can still compete if they land with HP advantage

**Recommended Loadout — Erangel**
• Slot 1: M416 / GROZA with 4x scope
• Slot 2: Kar98K or M24 for long-range picks
• Utility: 4 smokes, 2 grenades, 1 molotov
• Healing: 6 medkits, 10+ bandages, 4 energy drinks

## Miramar Map Strategy

**Key Drop Locations**
• Campo Militare: Mid-tier military loot, good for quick rotation teams
• Ciudad de Pecado: Tier-3 loot city, very contested
• El Pozo: Good loot, central position
• Hacienda del Patron: Famous hot drop, house camping very common
• Prison: Excellent loot, isolated position — commit or skip

**Rotation Strategy — Miramar**
• The map is rocky and open. Elevation is king.
• Ridgeline rotations: always hug cliff edges to avoid open exposure
• Vehicles are a liability in end zones — ditch them early
• Water tower positions (near El Azahar, Chumacera) offer strong sniping platforms
• Smoke rotations are essential when crossing the open desert areas
• Use vehicles only for displacement, not for stopping to fight

**Zone Play Tips**
• Final zones often spawn in the eastern plateau, prison island, or northwest desert
• Teams with bolt-action snipers dominate early and mid Miramar circles
• Aggression in Miramar is punished more than in Erangel — patience wins

**Recommended Loadout — Miramar**
• Slot 1: AKM or SLR with 6x scope for medium-long range
• Slot 2: Kar98K or Win94 for extended long range
• Utility: 4 smokes, 2 grenades
• Healing: 6 medkits, 10 bandages, 4 energy drinks

## Sanhok Map Strategy

**Key Drop Locations**
• Boot Camp: Best loot on Sanhok, extreme congestion — high-risk
• Ruins: Solid loot, unique vertical gameplay
• Paradise Resort: Tier-3 loot, constant fighting
• Docks: Moderate loot, early vantage for boat rotations
• Cave: Unique enclosed area, good surprise rotations

**Rotation Strategy — Sanhok**
• Sanhok is a small map — circles collapse fast
• Priority: Get inside circle immediately after initial engagement
• Don't loot greedily — get guns and move
• River rotations (boat) are fastest and cover most of the map
• Final zones compress extremely fast; teams need to be pre-positioned
• Avoid fighting until zone 3 if possible — let other teams bleed each other

**Zone Play Tips**
• The jungle terrain provides cover but reduces visibility — use this defensively
• Boot Camp finals are brutal — compound walls create crossfire chokepoints
• Use Ghillie Suits and prone positions in final circles

**Recommended Loadout — Sanhok**
• Slot 1: SCAR-L / M416 with 2x-3x scope (close-mid fights)
• Slot 2: Vector or UMP45 for CQB
• Utility: 5 smokes, 1 grenade
• Healing: 6 medkits, 10 bandages, 4 energy drinks

## General Competitive Tips

**Communication**
• Callouts should be directional: clock positions (e.g., "contact 9 o'clock, 200m")
• IGL confirms engagement before fragger pushes
• Support calls smoke placement before reviving

**Economy Management**
• Don't loot past zone 2 — time spent looting = positioning lost
• Early care packages are high risk — contest only if defended

**Late Game (Top 5 Teams)**
• Identify highest threat team and focus them first
• Avoid two-front fights
• Use smokes liberally in final circles — control information, not just positions
